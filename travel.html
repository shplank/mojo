Travel

Safety

Even though you’re fully vaccinated, precautions are necessary to help prevent exposure and transmission. At minimum, you must wear a mask over your nose and mouth on domestic flights—but that’s not enough. Based on our research, the following tips are strongly recommended:

  • Double-mask or wear an N95 mask.
  • Wear a face shield or glasses (yes, really).
  • Avoid eating or drinking on the plane, or eat when fewer people are unmasked, and keep your mask on between sips/bites.
  • Wash your hands often.
  • Carry hand sanitizer (TSA is allowing one liquid hand sanitizer container up to 12 ounces per passenger in carry-on bags until further notice).
  • Maintain physical distance as much as possible while in the airport, especially while eating/drinking.
  • Bring your own snacks and water bottle—fewer touchpoints, fewer germs.
  • Enroll in TSA PreCheck. It means less time standing in crowded lines AND not having to put all your belongings in a germy bin.
